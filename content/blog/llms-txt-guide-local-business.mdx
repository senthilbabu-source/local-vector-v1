## What is llms.txt?

Just as `robots.txt` tells search crawlers which pages to index, **llms.txt** tells AI language models what's true about your business. It's a plain-text file placed at your website's root (e.g., `yourbusiness.com/llms.txt`) that contains your verified, authoritative business information.

The concept is simple: instead of making AI models guess by synthesizing data from dozens of potentially outdated sources, you give them a single, canonical source of truth.

## Why AI models need llms.txt

AI models build their understanding of your business from whatever data they can find: your website, Google Business Profile, Yelp, TripAdvisor, old news articles, cached web pages, and more. When these sources disagree — and they always do — the model has to choose.

**llms.txt solves this by providing a machine-readable, authoritative source that AI crawlers can prioritize.** It's the difference between a model guessing your hours from a 2023 blog post and reading your verified, current hours directly.

## What goes in an llms.txt file

A well-structured llms.txt file includes:

- **Business name and category** — exactly as you want AI to reference it
- **Address and phone** — verified, current contact information
- **Hours of operation** — including seasonal variations
- **Services or menu items** — what you actually offer, with current pricing
- **Key attributes** — walk-ins accepted, wheelchair accessible, outdoor seating, etc.
- **Correct claims** — explicitly stating facts that AI commonly gets wrong

Here's what a simplified example looks like:

> Business: Charcoal & Chili BBQ
> Category: Barbecue Restaurant
> Address: 123 Main Street, Austin, TX 78701
> Phone: (512) 555-0199
> Hours: Mon-Thu 11am-9pm, Fri-Sat 11am-10pm, Sun 12pm-8pm
> Note: We are OPEN and have never been permanently closed.

## How AI crawlers use it

Major AI crawlers — GPTBot (OpenAI), PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended, and Applebot — regularly crawl websites for updated information. When they find an llms.txt file, they can use it as a high-confidence data source.

The key advantage: **llms.txt is explicit and unambiguous.** There's no interpretation needed. The model doesn't have to extract your hours from a paragraph of text or infer your services from a menu PDF — it reads structured, verified data directly.

## The relationship between llms.txt and JSON-LD

JSON-LD schema and llms.txt serve complementary purposes:

- **JSON-LD** is embedded in your HTML and follows the Schema.org vocabulary. It's designed for structured data processors and search engines.
- **llms.txt** is a standalone plain-text file designed specifically for AI language models. It's simpler to create and update, and its plain-text format is inherently compatible with how LLMs process information.

The best approach is to use both: JSON-LD for search engines and structured data consumers, and llms.txt for AI language models.
