## The Apple ecosystem is massive — and mostly ignored

When local businesses think about AI visibility, they think about ChatGPT and Google. Almost nobody thinks about Siri. That's a mistake.

Apple has over **2 billion active devices** worldwide. Every iPhone, iPad, Mac, Apple Watch, and Vision Pro has Siri built in. With Apple Intelligence rolling out in 2025-2026, Siri is evolving from a basic voice assistant into a context-aware AI that recommends businesses, answers questions, and takes action.

Yet most local businesses have never claimed their Apple Business Connect listing — the direct equivalent of Google Business Profile for Apple's ecosystem.

## What Apple Intelligence means for local businesses

Apple Intelligence is integrated at the operating system level. It appears in:

- **Siri** — voice and text queries about local businesses
- **Apple Maps** — the default navigation on every iPhone
- **Spotlight Search** — the universal search bar on every Apple device
- **Safari** — intelligent summaries and recommendations while browsing
- **Messages** — contextual suggestions when users discuss plans

When a user asks Siri "find me a good Italian restaurant nearby" or types a query into Spotlight, Apple Intelligence draws from Apple Maps data — which comes from Apple Business Connect for claimed businesses, or from third-party aggregators for unclaimed ones.

## The four signals Apple Intelligence uses

### 1. Apple Business Connect
Your verified business listing in Apple's ecosystem. Without it, Apple relies on data aggregators — the same ones that often have outdated information.

### 2. Applebot crawlability
Apple's web crawler needs access to your website. Check your robots.txt — many businesses unknowingly block Applebot, making their site invisible to Siri.

### 3. Structured data
JSON-LD schema on your website that Applebot can parse. The more structured data you provide, the richer Siri's answers about your business.

### 4. Cross-platform consistency
Apple Intelligence cross-references multiple sources. Consistent data across Apple Business Connect, your website, and major directories increases confidence in Siri's recommendations.

## How to check your Siri Readiness

Here's a quick self-assessment:

- **Have you claimed your Apple Business Connect listing?** If not, Apple is using third-party data about your business.
- **Is Applebot allowed in your robots.txt?** Look for `User-agent: Applebot` — if it's followed by `Disallow: /`, Siri can't read your site.
- **Do you have JSON-LD schema?** Check your website source for `<script type="application/ld+json">` — if it's not there, you're missing a major signal.
- **Are your hours and address consistent?** Compare your website, GBP, Yelp, and Apple Maps. Any discrepancy creates doubt.
