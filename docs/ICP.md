## Section 1: Primary ICP Summary (One-Liner)

> **The owner or marketing manager of a 1–3 location full-service restaurant in a competitive US urban or suburban market who already manages their Google Business Profile, suspects AI search is changing how customers find restaurants, and is willing to pay a monthly subscription to track and fix how AI represents their business.**

## Section 4: Psychographic Profile

The primary ICP is not a technologist. They are a **competitive restaurateur who thinks like a marketer.**

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Identity** | Proud of their restaurant; takes quality seriously; competitive by nature |
| **Worldview** | "We're better than our competitors — I just need more people to know it." |
| **Relationship with tech** | Adopts tools when they solve a real problem; skeptical of hype; doesn't want to learn a new interface for its own sake |
| **Relationship with Google** | Feels like they "figured it out" — claimed GBP, responds to reviews, has photos. Now AI is changing the rules and it feels unfair. |
| **Pain point framing** | Not "AI optimization" — that's jargon. Their frame is: "People are asking AI where to eat and my restaurant either isn't showing up or the AI is saying something wrong about us." |
| **Motivation** | More covers, more revenue, protecting the reputation they've built |
| **Fear** | A customer didn't come in because AI told them the wrong hours, or recommended a competitor instead |
| **Aspiration** | To be the restaurant AI recommends when someone asks for the best [cuisine] in [city] |

## Section 6: The Activation Moment

> **The moment a new user goes from "interesting tool" to "I need this" is the moment they see, for the first time, that AI is saying something specific and wrong about their restaurant.**

This is the product's core activation event. Everything in onboarding should be designed to get the user to this moment within their first session.

**What it looks like:**
- They run the ViralScanner or the AI Mistakes scan
- They see a specific AI hallucination: wrong hours, wrong address, wrong cuisine, or being listed as permanently closed
- The platform shows them the dollar impact: "AI sending customers the wrong hours costs an estimated $X/year"
- They understand: this is a real problem, it is happening right now, and this tool found it

**Secondary activation events:**
- They see a competitor is being mentioned by AI and they are not (SOV dashboard)
- They see their AI Health Score for the first time and it's a D or F
- They see the Revenue Impact calculator with their actual cover count and ticket size

**Time-to-activation target:** Under 5 minutes from signup. The ViralScanner is the top-of-funnel activation. The full onboarding scan is the in-product activation.

- **White-label:** Present LocalVector dashboards under their own brand

## Section 10: Anti-ICP — Who to Exclude

Saying no early saves support capacity and reduces churn. These are active disqualifiers.

| Segment | Why to Exclude | Signal |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| **No online presence** | Cannot benefit from AI visibility without a baseline digital footprint | No website, no GBP listing |
| **Food trucks** | No fixed address; AI location accuracy is less relevant | "We move around" |
| **Ghost kitchens / delivery-only** | No dining room reputation to protect; AI recommendation for dine-in is not their use case | Delivery-only operation |
| **Restaurants closing / in distress** | No budget; churn immediately | Google "permanently closed" status; distress signals |
| **Non-competitive markets** | Low competitive density = AI doesn't need to choose between options; SOV is less meaningful | Very small towns; monopoly-market restaurants |
| **Anti-tech owners** | Will not change behavior based on AI data; churn after first month | "I don't believe in this AI stuff" |
| **Enterprise chains (100+ locations)** | Requires enterprise contract, custom integrations, and dedicated support we can't yet provide | National brands; franchise groups >10 locations |
| **Non-English markets** | Product is US-English only; AI models are less accurate for non-English queries | Non-US / non-English primary |

## Section 11: Qualification Framework (Discovery Call)

Use these questions on every inbound or outbound conversation. Score each answer: 1 = disqualifier, 2 = neutral, 3 = strong signal.

| Question | What You're Scoring | Disqualifier | Strong Signal |
|----------|---------------------|-------------|---------------|
| "Have you ever Googled your restaurant on ChatGPT or Perplexity?" | Awareness of AI search | "What's that?" with no curiosity | "Yes, and the hours were wrong" / "We weren't mentioned" |
| "How many locations do you have?" | Plan fit | 0 / food truck / delivery-only | 1–3 (Growth), 4–10 (Agency) |
| "Do you currently pay for any digital marketing tools or services?" | Budget mindset | "No, I don't pay for anything online" | Names 2+ tools (Toast, OpenTable, Yelp ads) |
| "Who handles your Google Business Profile?" | Decision maker and ownership | "I don't know what that is" | "I manage it myself" or "our marketing person" |
| "Has a customer ever told you they found wrong information about your restaurant online?" | Pain awareness | "Never heard of that" | "Yes, happened last month" |
| "What would it mean for your business if AI recommended your restaurant every time someone asked for [cuisine] in [city]?" | Vision fit | Indifferent | Excited; starts imagining it; asks follow-up questions |

**Scoring:**
- 15–18 points: Tier 1 prospect — prioritize immediately
- 10–14 points: Tier 2 prospect — nurture with content
- Under 10: Deprioritize; route to free ViralScanner and wait for trigger event

## Section 13: Key Metrics to Track (Validate This ICP)

Once the first 10 paying customers are acquired, measure:

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **Restaurant type distribution** | >70% full-service or fast-casual | Validates primary ICP assumption |
| **Location count at signup** | Average 1–2 | Validates plan tier assumption |
| **Time to activation event** | <5 min from signup | ViralScanner → AI mistake found |
| **Trigger event at signup** | Ask on signup form: "What brought you here today?" | Validates trigger event ranking |
| **Churn reason (first 90 days)** | "Didn't see the value" = ICP miss; "Too expensive" = pricing issue | Distinguishes ICP problem from pricing problem |
| **Expansion from Growth → Agency** | >20% within 12 months | Validates multi-location aspiration |
| **NPS at 30 days** | >40 (Sean Ellis PMF threshold) | Overall ICP fit signal |
